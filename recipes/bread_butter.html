Bread Butter

Butter bread is an enriched bread with a rich, buttery, and slightly sweet taste. While readily available at your local bakery, you can also make the delicious bread at home.

  • 2 cups milk
  • 5 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 2 teaspoons salt>
  • 1 teaspoon instant yeast
  • 6 tablespoons unsalted butter
  1. 1 In the large bowl of a stand mixer, combine 2 cups milk, 5 cups all-purpose flour, 2 tablespoons granulated sugar, 2 teaspoons salt, and 1 tablespoon instant yeast. Mix the ingredients to form a dough, adding another ½ cup of flour if needed. Knead with the dough hook for 3 minutes at a low speed.
  2. 2 Add 6 tablespoons unsalted butter, and keep kneading with the dough hook until the butter is fully incorporated and the dough is elastic.
  3. 3 In a bowl covered with plastic wrap, let the dough rise for at least 90 minutes. It should double in size.
  4. 4 Divide the dough in half, shaping each half into a bread loaf on a floured surface or parchment paper. Place each loaf of dough into a lightly greased 8-inch loaf pan, seam side down. Let the dough rise again, covered, about 2 hours. The loaves should look puffy.
  5. Preheat the oven to 375 degrees Fahrenheit and uncover the loaves.
  6. 5 Bake the loaves for 30 minutes. They should look golden brown and produce a hollow sound when you tap them on the bottom. Cool them on a wire rack.
